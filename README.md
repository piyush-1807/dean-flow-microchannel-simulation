## Numerical Study of Dean Flow Formation in Curved Microchannels

## Project Overview

This project presents a numerical investigation of Dean flow formation in curved microchannels using ANSYS simulations.

Dean flow occurs when fluid passes through a curved channel, creating secondary vortices due to centrifugal forces and pressure gradients. The study compares Dean vortex formation in:

* U-Bent Microchannel
* Spiral Microchannel

## Objectives

* Develop 3D computational models in ANSYS
* Simulate Dean vortex formation
* Analyze the effect of Reynolds Number and Dean Number
* Compare U-bent and spiral channel performance

## Tools Used

* ANSYS Fluent
* CFD Simulation
* Fluid Mechanics
* Microfluidics

## Key Results

* Dean vortex strength increases with Dean Number.
* High-velocity regions shift toward the outer wall of the bend.
* Spiral and U-bent geometries exhibit distinct secondary flow characteristics.
